LUTS709 (built-in)
The S709 LUT is monitoring only — footage is always recorded as pure S-Log3. It is applied to the viewfinder and HDMI output only.
Because S709 is active, all monitoring tools — camera zebras and DC-A1 waveform/histogram — are measuring the S709 signal. Zebra levels are different from raw S-Log3 levels.
Zebra setup
Menu → Exposure/Colour → Zebra Setting
Levels calibrated for S709 signal. Do not use raw S-Log3 levels (41–52% / 85%) while S709 is active.

What they measure
C1 (61%)Bright skin tones — exposure target
C2 (77%)Approaching clip — white card sits at 77–83%
Exposure
Two modes
Controlled — studio / Godox lights
1
Hold white card in scene under your lights
2
Adjust until C2 (77%) fires on the white card
3
Confirm white card at 77–83% on DC-A1 waveform
4
Lock exposure. Set custom WB.
No ETTR needed. A7S III handles shadows cleanly at correct exposure.
Run and gun — changing light
1
Expose so C1 (61%) just fires on subject's skin
2
Check C2 (77%) is NOT firing on subject's face
3
If C2 fires on subject → pull exposure down
4
C2 on background only → acceptable
Optional ETTR: push until C1 disappears from skin, back off half a stop. ~+0.5 stop. Watch C2 doesn't fire on subject.
White balance
Never use full auto WB — drifts mid-clip
Controlled — custom WB
1
Set camera to S-Log3 at shooting exposure
2
Hold white card under your actual lights
3
Menu → Exposure/Colour → White Balance → Custom Setup
4
Point at card, press shutter → locked for entire shoot
Run and gun — AWB Lock Toggle
Setup once: Menu → Camera Settings 2 → Custom Key/Dial Settings → assign AWB Lock Toggle to a button
1
Point at subject or neutral area
2
Press AWB Lock → WB locks instantly
3
Hit record — WB won't drift mid-clip
4
As light changes: unlock → re-read → re-lock

Waveform & histogram
DC-A1 — reading the S709 signal
Waveform — how to use on set
1
Open waveform (F1)
2
Skin tone signal should sit at 61–65%
3
Confirm highlights not exceeding 90%
4
Confirm shadows not crushing below 10%
Run and gun: zebras = fast primary check, waveform = secondary confirm. Controlled: set with waveform first, verify with zebras.
Histogram — reading the scene
·Bulk in middle third (40–70%): well exposed
·Spike hard right edge: highlights clipping — pull down
·Spike hard left edge: shadows crushing — push up
·Bulk shifted far left: underexposed — check zebras
